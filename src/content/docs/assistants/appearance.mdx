## The Basics: Name & Greeting

Start by giving your assistant a name and writing their greeting message ‚Äì the first thing customers see when they open the chat. This is your assistant's first impression, so make it welcoming and clear!

<Aside type="tip" title="Name & Greeting Tips">
- Keep the name short and friendly (like "Alex" or "Support Team")
- The greeting should explain what your assistant can help with
- Use a friendly, conversational tone that matches your brand
- Think about your audience ‚Äì formal for business clients, casual for everyday customers
</Aside>

## Colors: Dressing for the Job

Pick colors that match your website and brand. Your assistant should look like they belong on your team!

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Assistant's Messages" icon="comment">
    Use your brand color here. Just make sure text is easy to read.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Customer's Messages" icon="pencil">
    Keep these neutral (gray or white) so they stand out from your assistant's replies.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Background" icon="document">
    Light backgrounds usually work best for easy reading.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Aside type="tip" title="Color Tips">
- Make sure text is easy to read against the background colors
- Test your colors on both light and dark website backgrounds
- Preview changes before saving to see how everything looks together
- Remember people with color blindness ‚Äì don't rely on color alone
</Aside>

### Color Examples

| Style | Assistant Color | Customer Color | Background | Works For |
|-------|----------------|----------------|------------|-----------|
| Professional | Navy Blue | Light Gray | White | Corporate, B2B |
| Friendly | Bright Blue | Light Gray | Off-white | Consumer apps |
| Bold | Brand Red | Dark Gray | White | Marketing sites |
| Minimal | Dark Gray | Light Gray | White | Clean, modern looks |

## See It Before You Save

The preview panel shows exactly what your chat widget will look like to customers. Any changes you make show up instantly, so you can experiment until it looks just right.

<Steps>
1. **Make a change** in the customization panel

2. **Check the preview** on the right side

3. **Try different backgrounds** using the preview options

4. **Save** when you're happy with how it looks
</Steps>

## Making It Look Professional

<Aside type="tip" title="Design Tips">
1. **Stay consistent** ‚Äì Match your website's overall look and feel
2. **Keep it readable** ‚Äì Make sure text is easy to read with good contrast
3. **Think mobile first** ‚Äì Most visitors will be on phones, so test there first
4. **Keep it simple** ‚Äì A clean look is more professional than over-designed
5. **Get feedback** ‚Äì Ask real customers what they think before finalizing
</Aside>
